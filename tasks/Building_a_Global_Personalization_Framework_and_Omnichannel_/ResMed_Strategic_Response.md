# Strategic Partnership: Personalization & Next Best Action Framework

*A Data-Informed Response to the ResMed Omnichannel Brief*

Thank you for sharing the brief and the all-time performance data. Building a personalization capability at scale requires moving beyond traditional campaign-based optimization toward a structured Next Best Action (NBA) and omnichannel approach. By looking at your current paid media performance, we can see exactly where an NBA framework will deliver the most immediate impact for ResMed.

Below is our perspective on your key questions, grounded in the performance data provided.

## 1. How we’ve approached personalization / NBA frameworks in other organizations

In our experience, successful personalization in healthcare is less about connecting every possible channel at once and more about identifying the critical "moments of truth" in the patient or HCP journey where a tailored message changes behavior [1]. An effective Next Best Action (NBA) framework functions as a decision engine that connects audience data, predictive models, and business objectives to generate prescriptive recommendations [2].

Looking at your data, you are currently spending $3.84M to drive a complex, multi-step funnel: from ad impression, to Sleep Assessment Start, to Sleep Assessment Complete, to Booked Call, and finally to a Platform Purchase.

An NBA framework would address the specific drop-offs and inefficiencies in this funnel:

*   **Predictive Channel Allocation:** Currently, Google Demand Gen ($502k spend) and YouTube ($226k spend) are driving zero attributed platform purchases. An NBA engine would recognize this lack of down-funnel conversion and automatically shift those impressions toward channels that are actually driving revenue, or change the objective for those channels to focus purely on top-of-funnel awareness rather than expecting direct response.
*   **Journey Orchestration:** You have 75,314 Sleep Assessment Completes, but only 1,290 Platform Purchases. An NBA framework would trigger personalized, sequential messaging (via email, SMS, or retargeting) to the ~74,000 individuals who completed the assessment but did not purchase, tailoring the message based on *why* they dropped off (e.g., price sensitivity vs. need for more medical information).
*   **Retargeting Optimization:** Your Facebook Retargeting is currently driving a high Cost Per Acquisition (CPA) of $2,393 compared to Google Shopping's $318 CPA. An NBA model would ensure that retargeting dollars are only spent on high-intent users, rather than broadly retargeting anyone who visited the site.

## 2. Our perspective on operationalizing this in a global, multi-market setup

Operationalizing a global personalization framework across multiple markets with shared partners requires a balance between centralized governance and localized execution. We recommend a Hub-and-Spoke Center of Excellence (CoE) model [3].

### The Hub-and-Spoke Operating Model

| Component | Responsibilities |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Global CoE (Hub)** | Defines standards, templates, measurement definitions, orchestration rules, and escalation paths. Owns the core technology stack and data architecture [3]. |
| **Local Affiliates (Spokes)** | Drives business strategy, creative direction, and channel execution tailored to local indications, regulations, and cultural nuances [3]. |
| **Shared Services** | Manages MLR/PRC coordination, marketing operations, CRM operations, and analytics enablement across markets [3]. |
| **Partners (Media/Agencies)** | Executes within the CoE’s standard operating procedures, QA checks, and reporting requirements [3]. |

For ResMed, this means standardizing how "success" is defined globally. Currently, different channels seem to be optimizing for different events (e.g., Google Non-Brand Search is highly efficient at driving Booked Calls at $55 each, while Facebook Prospecting costs $331 per Booked Call). The CoE would establish the global rules for how these channels interact, while local markets would tailor the actual creative.

## 3. Considerations for the Americas Experience

When applying this framework to the Americas, specific regional dynamics must be considered. The US market is characterized by a highly complex payer landscape, stringent regulatory environments (e.g., FDA advertising requirements, HIPAA), and a vast, diverse audience.

*   **Data Richness vs. Privacy:** The Americas often have access to richer third-party data sets. However, patient programs require strict adherence to HIPAA Privacy Rules when handling Protected Health Information (PHI) [3]. The NBA framework must be designed with privacy-by-design principles, ensuring that personalization does not cross the line into intrusive or non-compliant use of health data.
*   **Shared Partner Alignment:** Given the shared media buying and internal stakeholder landscape, establishing clear Service Level Agreements (SLAs) and build standards for agencies is critical. Partners must understand their QA and compliance responsibilities within the global CoE framework [3]. This ensures that media buying partners aren't just spending budgets in silos (as seen with the $729k spent on Demand Gen and YouTube with zero purchases), but are held accountable to full-funnel NBA metrics.

## 4. What a practical 3–6 month starting point could look like

Transitioning to a global NBA framework is a significant transformation. A practical 3-6 month roadmap should focus on establishing the foundation and delivering quick wins to build momentum, specifically targeting the inefficiencies highlighted in your data.

### Month 1: Discovery, Alignment, and Triage

*   **Stakeholder Alignment:** Conduct workshops with global and regional leaders to define the vision, objectives, and scope of the personalization initiative.
*   **Ecosystem Audit:** Assess the current state of data integration, technology stack, and channel capabilities.
*   **Immediate Triage:** Based on the data, immediately audit the tracking and attribution for Google Demand Gen, YouTube, and Facebook ASC. Together, these represent over $832k in spend with zero attributed purchases. We need to determine if this is a tracking failure or a genuine performance issue.

### Months 2-3: Foundation Building and Data Consolidation

*   **Customer 360 View:** Begin integrating key data sources (CRM, web analytics, media platforms) to build an initial Customer 360 view for a pilot segment [1].
*   **Process Standardization:** Establish the standardized campaign intake process and initial modular content guidelines [3].
*   **Define the "Drop-off" Pilot:** We recommend focusing the initial NBA pilot on the massive drop-off between "Sleep Assessment Complete" (75k users) and "Platform Purchase" (1.2k users). This is your highest-leverage opportunity.

### Months 4-6: Pilot Execution and Measurement

*   **Model Deployment:** Develop and deploy an initial predictive model to score the 74k non-purchasing assessment completers based on their likelihood to buy [2].
*   **Orchestration Activation:** Connect the decision engine to execution channels (e.g., personalized email flows, targeted SMS, and highly specific programmatic retargeting) [1].
*   **Measurement and Optimization:** Implement standardized analytics tracking. Review early performance data against the baseline CPA and ROAS metrics to prove the value of the NBA approach [3].
