*A Data-Informed Response to the ResMed Omnichannel Brief*

Thank you for sharing the brief and the fiscal year performance data. Before responding to your questions directly, we want to acknowledge something the YoY dashboard makes clear: **ResMed has already executed a massive and successful optimization between the previous period and the current fiscal year.** The channel mix has been restructured, and the cost of acquiring a qualified lead has improved dramatically. That context shapes everything we say below — this is not a conversation about fixing a broken program. It is a conversation about what comes next.

## 1. How we've approached personalization / NBA frameworks in other organizations

In our experience, the most effective personalization programs in healthcare are not built around campaigns — they are built around **the individual's journey and intent signals**. An NBA framework functions as a decision engine that continuously evaluates what the most relevant next action is for each person, across every channel, at every moment [1][2].

Looking at your YoY dashboard, ResMed's funnel has two distinct stories happening at once:

**The Top-of-Funnel Success Story:**
*   **Sleep Assessment Completes** are up **22,118%** (from 309 to 68,653).
*   **Cost per Sleep Assessment Complete** is down **99%** (from $3,425.58 to $19.27).
*   **Booked Calls** are up **183,132%** (from 28 to 51,305).
*   **Cost per Booked Call** is down **100%** (from $37,803.68 to $25.78).

**The Mid-to-Lower Funnel Gap:**
While you are acquiring high-intent leads at an extraordinarily efficient rate, the conversion rate from those actions to actual Platform Purchases is the primary bottleneck. You have generated 68,653 Sleep Assessment Completes and 51,305 Booked Calls this period, but only **698 Platform Purchases**.

This is precisely the problem an NBA framework solves. Rather than relying on a single retargeting pool or a generic email sequence, an NBA engine would:

- **Score each of the tens of thousands of non-purchasing leads** based on their behavioral signals to predict their likelihood to purchase and their most likely barrier.
- **Trigger personalized, sequential owned-channel follow-up** — tailored email, SMS, or direct outreach — based on where each individual is in their decision journey.
- **Coordinate paid retargeting** only for the highest-intent segment, rather than broadly retargeting the entire pool at scale.

## 2. Our perspective on operationalizing this in a global, multi-market setup

Operationalizing a global personalization framework across multiple markets with shared partners requires a balance between centralized governance and localized execution. We recommend a **Hub-and-Spoke Center of Excellence (CoE)** model [3].

### The Hub-and-Spoke Operating Model

| Component | Responsibilities |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Global CoE (Hub)** | Defines standards, templates, measurement definitions, orchestration rules, and escalation paths. Owns the core technology stack and data architecture [3]. |
| **Local Affiliates (Spokes)** | Drives business strategy, creative direction, and channel execution tailored to local indications, regulations, and cultural nuances [3]. |
| **Shared Services** | Manages marketing operations, CRM operations, and analytics enablement across markets [3]. |
| **Partners (Media/Agencies)** | Executes within the CoE's standard operating procedures, QA checks, and reporting requirements [3]. |

A CoE with a defined NBA decisioning layer makes reallocations and optimizations **automatically and continuously**, rather than in annual or quarterly planning cycles.

### Key Governance Pillars

A scalable CoE requires foundational pillars: standardized campaign intake, a modular content framework, orchestration and CRM governance, analytics standardization, and formal partner governance [3]. For ResMed specifically, the most urgent pillar is **analytics standardization** and defining what "conversion" means across all channels and partners, ensuring that the incredible top-of-funnel volume translates into bottom-line revenue.

## 3. Considerations for the Americas Experience

The Americas data tells a story of rapid, effective optimization — but also of a program that is still operating in a largely campaign-based, channel-siloed model. Several dynamics are specific to this market:

**The paid-to-owned gap is the defining challenge.** The data provided covers paid media performance. With 68,653 Sleep Assessment Completes this period and only 698 purchases, the question that matters most is: **what happens in owned channels after the assessment is completed?** If the answer is a generic email drip, that is the single highest-leverage area for personalization investment. If there is no structured owned-channel follow-up at all, that is the starting point for the entire NBA build.

**The shared partner and stakeholder model creates a coordination risk.** With different partners managing media buying, internal stakeholders across regions, and a global organization, there is a real risk that optimization decisions made in one channel are not visible to — or coordinated with — teams managing adjacent channels. The NBA framework must sit above the individual channel partners, with the CoE owning the decisioning rules that govern how each partner activates.

## 4. What a practical 3–6 month starting point could look like

Given where ResMed is today — incredible top-of-funnel efficiency, a massive pool of unconverted qualified leads, and a clear measurement gap in the mid-funnel — we recommend a starting point focused on **converting what you have already paid to acquire**, rather than building the full global framework from scratch.

### Month 1: Audit the Post-Assessment Journey

The first and most important question to answer is: what currently happens to someone who completes a Sleep Assessment or Books a Call but does not purchase? Map every touchpoint — paid retargeting, email, SMS, direct outreach — and identify where the journey ends. This audit will define the scope of the NBA pilot and is likely to surface quick wins that can be activated within weeks.

### Months 2–3: Build the Customer 360 and Define the Pilot

Begin integrating key data sources — CRM, web analytics, Sleep Assessment completion data, and media platform signals — to build an initial Customer 360 view for the post-assessment audience [1]. This does not require a full CDP build; a structured data layer connecting these sources is sufficient to begin scoring.

Define the pilot around a single, high-value journey: the path from Sleep Assessment Complete / Booked Call to first purchase. Select a specific market or audience segment (e.g., the Americas) to keep the scope manageable.

### Months 4–6: Deploy, Activate, and Measure

Develop and deploy an initial predictive model to score the non-purchasing leads based on their likelihood to convert and their most likely barrier [2]. Connect the model to execution channels — personalized email flows, targeted SMS, and tightly defined paid retargeting — and activate the first NBA-driven journeys [1].

Measure performance against the current baseline: 698 purchases from 68,653 completes. Even a modest improvement in the conversion rate across that volume represents significant revenue at scale.
