# Strategic Partnership: Personalization & Next Best Action Framework

*A Data-Informed Response to the ResMed Omnichannel Brief*

Thank you for sharing the brief and the performance data across periods. Before responding to your questions directly, we want to acknowledge something the data makes clear: **ResMed has already executed a significant and successful optimization between 2025 and 2026.** The channel mix has been restructured, inefficient spend has been paused, and the cost of acquiring a qualified lead has improved dramatically. That context shapes everything we say below — this is not a conversation about fixing a broken program. It is a conversation about what comes next.

---

## 1. How we've approached personalization / NBA frameworks in other organizations

In our experience, the most effective personalization programs in healthcare are not built around campaigns — they are built around **the individual's journey and intent signals**. An NBA framework functions as a decision engine that continuously evaluates what the most relevant next action is for each person, across every channel, at every moment [1][2].

Looking at your data, ResMed's funnel has three distinct stages, each with a different problem that personalization can solve:

| Funnel Stage | 2025 Performance | 2026 YTD Performance | Observation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Impression → Click** | CTR: 1.15%, CPM: $6.38 | CTR: 2.64%, CPM: $46.98 | 2026 is buying higher-intent inventory at a premium — a deliberate trade-off |
| **Click → Sleep Assessment Complete** | Cost/Complete: $372.55 | Cost/Complete: $19.27 | **95% improvement** — the top-of-funnel acquisition engine is now highly efficient |
| **Sleep Assessment Complete → Purchase** | 6,662 completes → 592 purchases (8.9% CVR) | 68,653 completes → 698 purchases (1.0% CVR) | **The critical gap** — volume of qualified leads has exploded 10x, but purchase conversion has collapsed |

The third row is the most important number in your data. In 2026 YTD, you are generating 68,653 Sleep Assessment Completes at just $19.27 each. That is an extraordinary lead generation result. But only 698 of those people have purchased — a 1.0% conversion rate, down from 8.9% in 2025. **You have built a highly efficient top-of-funnel machine and the mid-to-lower funnel has not kept pace.**

This is precisely the problem an NBA framework solves. Rather than relying on a single retargeting pool or a generic email sequence, an NBA engine would:

- **Score each of the 68,000 non-purchasing completers** based on their behavioral signals (time spent on assessment, device type, content engaged with, channel of origin) to predict their likelihood to purchase and their most likely barrier.
- **Trigger personalized, sequential owned-channel follow-up** — tailored email, SMS, or direct outreach — based on where each individual is in their decision journey.
- **Coordinate paid retargeting** only for the highest-intent segment, rather than broadly retargeting the entire completer pool at scale.

## 2. Our perspective on operationalizing this in a global, multi-market setup

Operationalizing a global personalization framework across multiple markets with shared partners requires a balance between centralized governance and localized execution. We recommend a **Hub-and-Spoke Center of Excellence (CoE)** model [3].

### The Hub-and-Spoke Operating Model

| Component | Responsibilities |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Global CoE (Hub)** | Defines standards, templates, measurement definitions, orchestration rules, and escalation paths. Owns the core technology stack and data architecture [3]. |
| **Local Affiliates (Spokes)** | Drives business strategy, creative direction, and channel execution tailored to local indications, regulations, and cultural nuances [3]. |
| **Shared Services** | Manages marketing operations, CRM operations, and analytics enablement across markets [3]. |
| **Partners (Media/Agencies)** | Executes within the CoE's standard operating procedures, QA checks, and reporting requirements [3]. |

The spend mix shift between 2025 and 2026 illustrates exactly why this governance model matters. Between 2025 and 2026, Google Demand Gen ($503k) and YouTube ($224k) — which together represented nearly 30% of 2025 spend with zero attributed purchases — were paused entirely. Google Performance Max was scaled from 2.7% to 27.6% of spend. These are the right decisions, but they appear to have been made reactively. A CoE with a defined NBA decisioning layer would make these reallocations **automatically and continuously**, rather than in annual or quarterly planning cycles.

### Key Governance Pillars

A scalable CoE requires five foundational pillars: standardized campaign intake, a modular content framework, orchestration and CRM governance, analytics standardization, and formal partner governance [3]. For ResMed specifically, the most urgent pillar is **analytics standardization** — the data shows that Facebook ASC ($81k in 2026 YTD) and Google Search Unmapped ($15k) are still generating spend with zero purchase attribution. Before scaling the NBA framework globally, the measurement infrastructure must be airtight.

## 3. Considerations for the Americas Experience

The Americas data tells a story of rapid, effective optimization — but also of a program that is still operating in a largely campaign-based, channel-siloed model. Several dynamics are specific to this market:

**The paid-to-owned gap is the defining challenge.** The data provided covers paid media performance only. With 68,653 Sleep Assessment Completes in 2026 YTD and only 698 purchases, the question that matters most is: **what happens in owned channels after the assessment is completed?** If the answer is a generic email drip, that is the single highest-leverage area for personalization investment. If there is no structured owned-channel follow-up at all, that is the starting point for the entire NBA build.

**Channel efficiency varies dramatically and the data supports clear prioritization.** In 2026 YTD, Google Shopping delivers a 3.19x ROAS and Google Brand Search delivers 2.38x — the only channels with ROAS above 1.0x. Google Performance Max is generating Booked Calls at $14.58 each. These channels should be protected and scaled. By contrast, Facebook Prospecting is generating purchases at $8,952 CPA in 2026 YTD — a significant deterioration from $3,219 in 2025. An NBA framework would automatically deprioritize Facebook Prospecting for purchase-intent audiences and redirect it toward upper-funnel awareness objectives where its CPM of $2.03 (2025) makes it genuinely cost-effective.

**The shared partner and stakeholder model creates a coordination risk.** With different partners managing media buying, internal stakeholders across regions, and a global organization, there is a real risk that optimization decisions made in one channel are not visible to — or coordinated with — teams managing adjacent channels. The NBA framework must sit above the individual channel partners, with the CoE owning the decisioning rules that govern how each partner activates.

## 4. What a practical 3–6 month starting point could look like

Given where ResMed is today — strong top-of-funnel efficiency, a large pool of unconverted qualified leads, and a clear measurement gap in the mid-funnel — we recommend a starting point focused on **converting what you have already paid to acquire**, rather than building the full global framework from scratch.

### Month 1: Audit the Post-Assessment Journey

The first and most important question to answer is: what currently happens to someone who completes a Sleep Assessment but does not purchase? Map every touchpoint — paid retargeting, email, SMS, direct outreach — and identify where the journey ends. This audit will define the scope of the NBA pilot and is likely to surface quick wins that can be activated within weeks.

In parallel, conduct a measurement audit to resolve the attribution gaps (Facebook ASC, Google Search Unmapped) and establish a shared definition of what "conversion" means across all channels and partners.

### Months 2–3: Build the Customer 360 and Define the Pilot

Begin integrating key data sources — CRM, web analytics, Sleep Assessment completion data, and media platform signals — to build an initial Customer 360 view for the post-assessment audience [1]. This does not require a full CDP build; a structured data layer connecting these sources is sufficient to begin scoring.

Define the pilot around a single, high-value journey: the path from Sleep Assessment Complete to first purchase. Select a specific market or audience segment (e.g., the Americas, or a specific product line) to keep the scope manageable.

### Months 4–6: Deploy, Activate, and Measure

Develop and deploy an initial predictive model to score the non-purchasing assessment completers based on their likelihood to convert and their most likely barrier [2]. Connect the model to execution channels — personalized email flows, targeted SMS, and tightly defined paid retargeting — and activate the first NBA-driven journeys [1].

Measure performance against the 2026 YTD baseline: 1.0% Sleep Assessment Complete-to-Purchase conversion rate and $1,893 overall CPA. Even a modest improvement in the conversion rate across 68,000 completers represents significant revenue at scale.
