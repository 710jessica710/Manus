# Strategic Partnership: Personalization & Next Best Action Framework

*A Data-Informed Response to the ResMed Omnichannel Brief*

Thank you for sharing the brief and the all-time performance data, along with the 2025 YTD breakout. Building a personalization capability at scale requires moving beyond traditional campaign-based optimization toward a structured Next Best Action (NBA) and omnichannel approach. 

By comparing your 2024 performance against your 2025 YTD data, we can see a powerful story of optimization already underway—and exactly where an NBA framework will deliver the next leap in performance.

Below is our perspective on your key questions, grounded in what the data reveals.

## 1. How we’ve approached personalization / NBA frameworks in other organizations

In our experience, successful personalization in healthcare is less about connecting every possible channel at once and more about identifying the critical "moments of truth" in the patient or HCP journey where a tailored message changes behavior [1]. An effective Next Best Action (NBA) framework functions as a decision engine that connects audience data, predictive models, and business objectives to generate prescriptive recommendations [2].

Looking at your data, ResMed has made incredible strides in top-of-funnel efficiency in 2025:
*   **Cost per Sleep Assessment Complete** plummeted from **$378.63 in 2024** to just **$19.27 in 2025**.
*   **Cost per Booked Call** dropped from **$1,998.76 in 2024** to **$25.78 in 2025**.
*   **Overall CPA** improved from **$4,260 in 2024** to **$1,893 in 2025**.

However, while you are acquiring high-intent leads much more efficiently, the **conversion rate from Sleep Assessment to Purchase remains the primary bottleneck**. You have generated 68,653 Sleep Assessment Completes in 2025, but only 698 Platform Purchases. 

An NBA framework would address this specific gap:

*   **Journey Orchestration for the "Lost" 68k:** An NBA framework would trigger personalized, sequential messaging (via email, SMS, or retargeting) to the tens of thousands of individuals who completed the assessment in 2025 but did not purchase. The engine would tailor the message based on *why* they dropped off (e.g., price sensitivity vs. need for more medical information).
*   **Predictive Channel Allocation:** In 2024, you spent $502k on Google Demand Gen and $226k on YouTube with zero attributed platform purchases. We note that in 2025, spend on these channels was paused or shifted. An NBA engine would automate this kind of reallocation in real-time, recognizing a lack of down-funnel conversion and shifting impressions toward channels that drive revenue (like Google Shopping, which drove a 3.19x ROAS in 2025).
*   **Retargeting Optimization:** Your Facebook Retargeting efficiency improved drastically in 2025 (ROAS up to 1.87x from 0.10x in 2024). An NBA model would further optimize this by ensuring retargeting dollars are only spent on the highest-intent users from that pool of 68k assessment completers.

## 2. Our perspective on operationalizing this in a global, multi-market setup

Operationalizing a global personalization framework across multiple markets with shared partners requires a balance between centralized governance and localized execution. We recommend a Hub-and-Spoke Center of Excellence (CoE) model [3].

### The Hub-and-Spoke Operating Model

| Component | Responsibilities |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Global CoE (Hub)** | Defines standards, templates, measurement definitions, orchestration rules, and escalation paths. Owns the core technology stack and data architecture [3]. |
| **Local Affiliates (Spokes)** | Drives business strategy, creative direction, and channel execution tailored to local indications, regulations, and cultural nuances [3]. |
| **Shared Services** | Manages MLR/PRC coordination, marketing operations, CRM operations, and analytics enablement across markets [3]. |
| **Partners (Media/Agencies)** | Executes within the CoE’s standard operating procedures, QA checks, and reporting requirements [3]. |

For ResMed, this means standardizing how "success" is defined globally. Currently, different channels optimize for different events. The CoE would establish the global rules for how these channels interact, while local markets would tailor the actual creative.

## 3. Considerations for the Americas Experience

When applying this framework to the Americas, specific regional dynamics must be considered. The US market is characterized by a highly complex payer landscape, stringent regulatory environments (e.g., FDA advertising requirements, HIPAA), and a vast, diverse audience.

*   **Data Richness vs. Privacy:** The Americas often have access to richer third-party data sets. However, patient programs require strict adherence to HIPAA Privacy Rules when handling Protected Health Information (PHI) [3]. The NBA framework must be designed with privacy-by-design principles, ensuring that personalization does not cross the line into intrusive or non-compliant use of health data.
*   **Shared Partner Alignment:** Given the shared media buying and internal stakeholder landscape, establishing clear Service Level Agreements (SLAs) and build standards for agencies is critical. Partners must understand their QA and compliance responsibilities within the global CoE framework [3]. This ensures that media buying partners are held accountable to full-funnel NBA metrics, rather than optimizing in silos.

## 4. What a practical 3–6 month starting point could look like

Transitioning to a global NBA framework is a significant transformation. A practical 3-6 month roadmap should focus on establishing the foundation and delivering quick wins to build momentum, specifically targeting the conversion gap highlighted in your 2025 data.

### Month 1: Discovery, Alignment, and Funnel Audit

*   **Stakeholder Alignment:** Conduct workshops with global and regional leaders to define the vision, objectives, and scope of the personalization initiative.
*   **Ecosystem Audit:** Assess the current state of data integration, technology stack, and channel capabilities.
*   **The "Lost Completers" Audit:** Immediately audit the journey of the 68k users who completed a Sleep Assessment in 2025. Where do they go? What communications do they currently receive?

### Months 2-3: Foundation Building and Data Consolidation

*   **Customer 360 View:** Begin integrating key data sources (CRM, web analytics, media platforms) to build an initial Customer 360 view for a pilot segment [1].
*   **Process Standardization:** Establish the standardized campaign intake process and initial modular content guidelines [3].
*   **Define the Pilot:** We recommend focusing the initial NBA pilot exclusively on the drop-off between "Sleep Assessment Complete" and "Platform Purchase." Since you have already solved the top-of-funnel acquisition cost in 2025, this is your highest-leverage opportunity for revenue growth.

### Months 4-6: Pilot Execution and Measurement

*   **Model Deployment:** Develop and deploy an initial predictive model to score the non-purchasing assessment completers based on their likelihood to buy [2].
*   **Orchestration Activation:** Connect the decision engine to execution channels (e.g., personalized email flows, targeted SMS, and highly specific programmatic retargeting) [1].
*   **Measurement and Optimization:** Implement standardized analytics tracking. Review early performance data against the 2025 baseline CPA ($1,893) and ROAS (0.19x) to prove the value of the NBA approach [3].
