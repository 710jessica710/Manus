# Strategic Partnership: Global Personalization & Next Best Action Framework

This document outlines a strategic perspective on building a global personalization capability at scale, moving beyond traditional campaign-based optimization toward a structured Next Best Action (NBA) and omnichannel approach across paid and owned ecosystems for HCP and patient audiences.

## 1. Approach to Personalization and NBA Frameworks

Personalization in the pharmaceutical industry is the process of using data to engage healthcare providers (HCPs) and patients with relevant messages specific to their interests and needs at the right time [1]. An effective Next Best Action (NBA) framework functions as a decision engine that connects audience data, predictive models, and business objectives to generate prescriptive recommendations [2].

A robust NBA framework typically consists of several core components:

*   **Data Integration and Audience Profiling:** Consolidating data from all available sources to build a comprehensive 360-degree profile. For HCPs, this includes CRM entries, past engagement history, content metadata, and third-party data such as claims or prescription trends [2].
*   **Segmentation and Targeting:** Filtering the audience universe into cohorts based on objective relevance and prioritizing those who are eligible and likely to engage [2].
*   **Predictive Modeling:** Utilizing machine learning models to score possible actions. These models predict which individuals to engage, the optimal channel (e.g., email, call, face-to-face), the preferred content type, and the best timing for outreach [2].
*   **Decision Engine:** Combining model scores with business rules and constraints (such as contact frequency limits) to rank potential actions by estimated value, ultimately recommending the single "best" action [2].
*   **Workflow Integration:** Delivering recommendations directly into the daily workflows of commercial teams, such as through CRM dashboards, providing explainable and actionable insights [2].

> "An NBA system is essentially a decision engine that links HCP data, predictive models, and business objectives to prescriptive recommendations." [2]

## 2. Operationalizing in a Global, Multi-Market Setup

Operationalizing a global personalization framework across multiple markets with shared partners requires a balance between centralized governance and localized execution. A Hub-and-Spoke Center of Excellence (CoE) model is highly effective in this context [3].

### The Hub-and-Spoke Operating Model

| Component | Responsibilities |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Global CoE (Hub)** | Defines standards, templates, measurement definitions, orchestration rules, and escalation paths. Owns the core technology stack and data architecture [3]. |
| **Local Affiliates (Spokes)** | Drives business strategy, creative direction, and channel execution tailored to local indications, regulations, and cultural nuances [3]. |
| **Shared Services** | Manages MLR/PRC coordination, marketing operations, CRM operations, and analytics enablement across markets [3]. |
| **Partners (Media/Agencies)** | Executes within the CoE’s standard operating procedures, QA checks, and reporting requirements [3]. |

### Key Governance Pillars

To ensure scalable execution and alignment across the complex organization, several governance pillars must be established:

1.  **Standardized Campaign Intake:** A unified process for campaign requests that captures audience, objectives, channel mix, data needs, and measurement plans to manage capacity and ensure compliance [3].
2.  **Modular Content Framework:** Treating content as reusable building blocks (core claims, safety language, channel wrappers) with strict metadata standards to reduce rework and accelerate local adaptation [3].
3.  **Orchestration and CRM Governance:** Defining clear rules for journey logic, identity management, consent flags, and frequency capping, ensuring that local execution adheres to global contact policies [3].
4.  **Analytics Standardization:** Establishing common definitions for business outcomes, operational KPIs, and channel metrics to ensure performance is measured consistently across all markets [3].

## 3. Considerations for the Americas Experience

When applying this framework to the Americas, specific regional dynamics must be considered. The US market, for instance, is characterized by a highly complex payer landscape, stringent regulatory environments (e.g., FDA advertising requirements, HIPAA), and a vast, diverse HCP population.

*   **Regulatory Compliance:** Omnichannel execution must align with FDA guidance on internet and social media promotion, ensuring risk information is appropriately presented alongside benefit information [3]. Patient programs require strict adherence to HIPAA Privacy Rules when handling Protected Health Information (PHI) [3].
*   **Data Richness:** The Americas often have access to richer third-party data sets (e.g., claims data, EHR signals) compared to other regions. The NBA framework should leverage this depth to drive more sophisticated predictive modeling [2].
*   **Shared Partner Alignment:** Given the shared media buying and internal stakeholder landscape, establishing clear Service Level Agreements (SLAs) and build standards for agencies is critical. Partners must understand their QA and compliance responsibilities within the global CoE framework [3].

## 4. A Practical 3–6 Month Starting Point

Transitioning to a global NBA framework is a significant transformation. A practical 3-6 month roadmap should focus on establishing the foundation and delivering quick wins to build momentum.

### Month 1: Discovery and Alignment

*   **Stakeholder Alignment:** Conduct workshops with global and regional leaders to define the vision, objectives, and scope of the personalization initiative.
*   **Ecosystem Audit:** Assess the current state of data integration, technology stack, and channel capabilities. Identify gaps in the paid and owned ecosystem.
*   **Charter Definition:** Draft the one-page charter for the Omnichannel CoE, defining decision rights, roles, and the hub-and-spoke model [3].

### Months 2-3: Foundation Building

*   **Data Consolidation:** Begin integrating key data sources (CRM, web analytics, email engagement) to build an initial Customer 360 view for a pilot segment [1].
*   **Process Standardization:** Establish the standardized campaign intake process and initial modular content guidelines [3].
*   **Pilot Selection:** Select a specific brand, therapeutic area, or high-value customer journey (e.g., a new product launch or key patient support program) for the initial NBA pilot.

### Months 4-6: Pilot Execution and Measurement

*   **Model Deployment:** Develop and deploy initial predictive models (e.g., channel affinity or content preference) for the pilot segment [2].
*   **Orchestration Activation:** Connect the decision engine to the selected execution channels (e.g., email and field force CRM) [1].
*   **Measurement and Optimization:** Implement standardized analytics tracking. Review early performance data, gather feedback from the field force, and iterate on the decision rules [3].
