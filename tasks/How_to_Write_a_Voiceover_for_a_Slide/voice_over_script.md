# Voice-Over Script: Blended Monthly Booking and Budget Pacing

**Slide Title:** Blended: Monthly Booking and Budget Pacing

---

**[Opening]**
"Let's move on to our Blended Monthly Booking and Budget Pacing slide, where we can review our performance across key channels for the month. We'll start by looking at our new patient bookings and then dive into our budget utilization."

**[Bookings Section]**
"Looking at the top table for 'Bookings By Channel, Monthly,' our total goal for new patient monthly bookings was 9,114. We achieved 7,526 actual bookings, putting us at 83% of our monthly goal. 

Breaking this down by channel:
*   **Google Search** continues to be our strongest driver. Against a goal of 8,404, we secured 7,189 bookings, hitting 86% of our target.
*   **Bing Search** brought in 320 bookings against a goal of 610, reaching 52% of its target.
*   **Facebook** saw 17 actual bookings against a goal of 100, which is 17% of the monthly goal."

**[Budget Section]**
"Now, shifting our focus to the bottom table, 'Spend By Channel, Monthly.' We are currently 86.67% through the month. 

Overall, our total budget is $1.83 million. Our month-to-date spend is just over $1.55 million, which means we have utilized 85% of our total budget. This aligns very closely with where we are in the month.

Looking at the individual channels:
*   **Google Search** spend is at roughly $1.43 million out of the $1.65 million budget, representing 87% utilization.
*   **Facebook** spend is at $46,723 out of a $60,000 budget, which is 78% of the allocated spend.
*   **Bing Search** is pacing a bit slower, with $69,628 spent out of the $120,000 budget, putting it at 58% utilization."

**[Conclusion/Summary]**
"In summary, our overall spend is pacing efficiently and perfectly in line with the time elapsed this month at 85% utilization versus 86.67% of the month passed. While Google Search is performing robustly in both spend and booking volume, we have opportunities to optimize our Facebook and Bing campaigns to drive higher booking conversion rates as we close out the month. 

Are there any questions on these figures before we move to the next slide?"
