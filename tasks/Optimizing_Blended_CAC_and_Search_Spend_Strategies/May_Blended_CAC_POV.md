# PlushCare May 2025: Blended CAC & Search Spend POV

**Author:** Manus AI  
**Date:** May 2025  

## Executive Summary

The current May pacing reveals a critical constraint: Facebook is expected to spend its allocated ~$60,000 but will only deliver ~40 bookings, resulting in a highly inefficient CPA of ~$1,500. This creates a significant "drag" on the overall budget efficiency. 

To achieve the total monthly goal of 8,360 bookings while maintaining a blended CAC of ≤$200, **Search (Google and Bing) must not only absorb the 60 missing bookings from Facebook but also operate at a combined efficiency of $193.75 CPA** to offset Facebook's overspend. 

The primary lever to pull is Bing, which is currently under-pacing at 42% of its monthly volume goal. This POV outlines how far we can push Bing spend and volume while holding the $200 blended CAC line.

---

## The Mathematical Reality

The $200 blended CAC target on 8,360 bookings creates a strict maximum spend ceiling. Facebook's inefficiency consumes a disproportionate amount of that budget.

| Metric | Target / Assumption | Implication |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Total Booking Target** | 8,360 | The North Star volume goal. |
| **Max Total Spend** | $1,672,000 | Calculated as 8,360 bookings × $200 blended CAC. |
| **Facebook Contribution** | 40 bookings @ $60,000 | Locks in $60K spend for minimal volume ($1,500 CPA). |
| **Search Volume Need** | 8,320 bookings | Search must cover its own goal plus FB's 60-booking shortfall. |
| **Max Search Budget** | $1,612,000 | The remaining budget after FB takes its $60K. |
| **Required Search CPA** | **$193.75** | Google and Bing combined *must* hit this efficiency to succeed. |

Because Google is currently operating at ~$201 CPA, **Bing must be leveraged aggressively at a lower CPA to bring the combined Search average down to $193.75.**

---

## Scenario Analysis: Pushing Bing Volume

To determine how far we can push Bing, we modeled several volume scenarios. In all scenarios, we assume Facebook is fixed (40 bookings / $60K) and we solve for the required Google and Bing dynamics to hit exactly a $200 blended CAC.

**Key Insight:** If Google can improve slightly to a $198 CPA, we unlock significant headroom to push Bing volume up to 1,200 bookings while allowing Bing's CPA to rise to $175. 

| Scenario | Bing Volume | Bing CPA Target | Bing Spend | Google Volume | Required Google CPA | Blended CAC |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Conservative** | 700 | $150 | $105,000 | 7,620 | $198 | $200 |
| **Base Case ★** | **800** | **$155** | **$124,000** | **7,520** | **$198** | **$200** |
| **Stretch** | 900 | $160 | $144,000 | 7,420 | $198 | $200 |
| **Aggressive** | 1,000 | $165 | $165,000 | 7,320 | $198 | $200 |
| **Max Push** | 1,100 | $170 | $187,000 | 7,220 | $197 | $200 |
| **Max Push+** | 1,200 | $175 | $210,000 | 7,120 | $197 | $200 |

### Sensitivity: What if Google CPA remains at $210?

If Google cannot improve efficiency and remains at its assumed $210 CPA, the math becomes highly restrictive. Because Google would be eating more of the budget, Bing would be forced to operate at unrealistically low CPAs to compensate:

* To hit 800 Bing bookings with Google at $210, **Bing CPA would have to drop to $41**.
* To hit 1,000 Bing bookings with Google at $210, **Bing CPA would have to drop to $75**.

**Conclusion:** We cannot maintain a $200 blended CAC if Google stays at $210 *and* Facebook comes in at $1,500. Google must be optimized down to the $197–$198 range to give Bing the breathing room to scale volume at a realistic $155–$175 CPA.

---

## Strategic Recommendations

Based on the scenario modeling, here is the recommended approach for the remainder of May:

1. **Optimize Google for Efficiency First ($198 Target):** 
   Google is currently driving the vast majority of volume, but at $201–$210, it is too expensive given the Facebook drag. We must tighten Google targeting, pausing inefficient non-brand terms, to force the CPA down to ~$198. This is the prerequisite for scaling Bing.

2. **Execute the "Base Case" for Bing (800 Bookings @ $155 CPA):**
   We recommend pushing Bing spend to **~$124,000** to capture 800 bookings. We can allow the Bing CPA goal to float up slightly from $150 to **$155**. This 3% CPA leniency should allow algorithms to capture more marginal volume while keeping the overall math intact.

3. **Establish a Bing "Stretch" Protocol:**
   If Google successfully achieves a $198 CPA early, we can push Bing further into the "Aggressive" scenario. We can authorize Bing spend up to **$165,000** to chase 1,000 bookings, allowing the Bing CPA to rise to **$165**. 

4. **Isolate Facebook:**
   Facebook is operating as a sunk cost for May. Do not attempt to force scale on Facebook; let it spend its $60K to capture the 40 bookings, but rely entirely on Search to manage the blended CAC.
